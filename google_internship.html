Google UX Design Internship


Background

For the Summer 2019, I sought a unique internship opportunity where I could prosper as a UX designer who can collaborate with new technology to deliver meaningful interactions. Therefore, I applied for a UX design intern role at Google, which I believed could offer me a unique experience. Thankfully, I received a call from Pritam Pebam, my intern host, and learned about what he and Google Lens team could offer. The project was about creating a new transit experience using machine perception and AR (Augmented Reality) capabilities. As a person who was always interested in how artificial intelligence could leverage our daily life, I was extremely thrilled to learn about this unique opportunity. Therefore, I expressed my excitement to my intern host. For the Summer 2019, I flew to Mountain View, California and spent 3 months interning as a UX designer with my Google Lens team at PR55.

Disclaimer

Unfortunately, since my internship is under NDA, I am unable to share any of details about this project.

Internship Project with Google Lens

As a part of the Google Lens team, I focused on creating a new transit experience for tourists using train system with computer vision and AR capabilities. Starting a new project from scratch, my primary goals were to identify train transit use cases and deliver directional concept designs and mocks.

Google Lens

A visual browser that utilizes computer vision and AR capabilities

Google Lens is a visual browser that utilizes computer vision to identify objects and AR capabilities to provide information in a real-world context. During my internship, I used these new technological trends to help provide value for the tourists using the train system.

My Role

As a UX design intern, I…

  • Established a profound foundation for a new Google Lens experience for 40 million tourists to help reduce transit anxiety by translating image or text and providing first person navigation
  • Discovered and verified a new Google Lens transit opportunity by conducting user study with 8 internal Googlers and observing tourists using NY metro
  • Expanded our team’s cross-functional team network by proactively reaching out to other Google teams for future collaboration
  • Influenced product managers, designers, researchers and engineers across Daydream and Google by sharing my insights and discoveries through UX snippets and share-out events

Takeaways

1. Collaboration starts from small talk and opens up new possibilities

One of my biggest takeaways was the Googlers. With such an open and collaborative culture, I was able to build a relationship with 40+ different Googlers with different expertise and teams. Talking and discussing with many different Googlers, I realized that small talk is very crucial in a professional world. Casual conversations during lunch or social events didn’t seem significant. In fact, it was like planting a seed, and they helped me to open up new opportunities. When I was stuck with a problem, I was able to reach out to a particular Googler who could provide me with expert feedback. Also, starting from small talk, I was able to establish connections with Googlers in different offices, such as New York and Tokyo. This eventually led to establishing a cross-functional team network within our team and creating an opportunity to travel to the New York office to leverage our product.

2. Prioritization and talking to the experts help handle ambiguities and challenges

At the start of my project, there were a lot of ambiguities in our problem scope and approach. Therefore, I proactively reached out to many Googlers in different teams to gain more understanding from different perspectives. From their feedback, I was able to understand their design challenges and how their approach to solve the problem. By understanding the constraints and limitations of each product, I could measure the values that only Lens could provide for the users. From this experience, I realized the power of sharing culture, which can not only help make a better decision but also draw a borderline to focus on the right problem.

3. Understanding the audience and utilizing visuals help better communicate my intent and insights

Throughout my internship, I strived to leverage my presentation skills to clearly communicate the intentions and purposes of my deliverables. After watching many Googlers’ presentations, I learned that understanding the audience is crucial. Depending on their interests, they might want to see a specific feature or a whole experience from my deliverables. Therefore, understanding their expectations helped me to be flexible to change the format of my deck. Another significant lesson I learned was the importance of delivering the key points by utilizing visuals. From the presentations I watched, I noticed that Googlers are extremely good at using visuals to aid their words. Therefore, when I was creating my deck, I carefully chose images and gifs that could support my idea.

Impact on my future career

Further exploration on AR and AI

As the current technology trends in AR and artificial intelligence are flourishing, my internship was a great opportunity to learn about the capabilities and limitations of the new domains. From this experience, I could develop my interest in AR and AI even more. Therefore, I decided to continue exploring and researching how I can use these superpowers to provide useful information in a real-context, as well as superpowers to utilize computer vision.

My internship experience with the Google Lens team was incomparable with other UX experiences I had throughout my career. This is especially true, because of all the Googlers I met during my internship. When I asked for a coffee chat or a meeting to get some feedback, not even one person said “no.” This amazing experience helped me grow not only as a designer but also as a person and a collaborator. I will always remember every single one of the Googlers I met. I especially want to thank my thoughtful intern host, Pritam for offering me this amazing journey at Google.

Thank you for your time to go through my journey!
