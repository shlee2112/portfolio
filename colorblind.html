Colorblind Traffic Light


Developing signals for colorblind drivers

Overview

A colorblind traffic light that communicates with distinguishable shapes and shows how much time remains.

Year: 2015

Duration: 2 months

Tools: Arduino, Rhino 3D, Keyshot, Laser Cutter, CNC Router, Vaccum Forming

Overall Process

Initial Research - Problem Identification – Approach – Ideation – Concept Development – Prototyping – Technical Development – Integration

Initial Research

How colorblind drivers perceive traffic lights

Worldwide, we have about 8% colorblind people who simply cannot make judgements through color. While, it seems 8% doesn’t look like a big number. However, when we look at statistics from the U.S. Department of Transportation, they claim that there are more than 250 million licensed drivers in the United States.(1) This shows that about 20 million drivers with color deficiencies are driving which poses a threat to other drivers.

20 million US drivers with color deficiencies

Different Configuration

It is said that colorblind drivers simply memorize top to bottom and left to right configuration. However, this doesn’t always apply. The configuration is different in many countries.

Children watching over their parents driving

The drivers bring back their memory as a child watching over their parents drive or even watching the traffic. Comparing similar environments, drivers instinctively know when to stop even when they are first driving.

How much the drivers have to consider in just a few seconds

The drivers have to consider light color, distance to light, auto speed and presence of police, pedestrian, lead car and cross traffic. This shows how complex and how much the drivers have to think over in just a few seconds.

How can I connect the colorblind drivers?

Design Approach

Shapes

Regardless of giving colors, the shapes can be distinguishable if there are far apart enough that people can recognize the shapes.

How can I get rid the driver’s anxiety to stop at a right time?

Countdown

Knowing remaining green light time will be easier for the drivers to stop than waiting for green light to turn into amber light. It gives psychological relief to the drivers.

Ideation

"Hourglass" interface to give a better recognition to the drivers.

Concept Development

Silent Presence

I used laser cutter to explore the interface of traffic light.

Prototyping

To make an accurate model, I decided to use CNC router. Therefore, I generated a 3D model using Rhino.

I CNC routed MDF and vacuum formed clear and white polystyrene sheets. Considering mass production, I choose this way to make prototypes.

Development

Coding simple C using Arduino, I produced the countdown feature and the appropriate shapes for the colorblind drivers.

Integration

Finally, I integrated the physical parts, LED and Arduino together.

Final Deliverable

A colorblind traffic light that communicates with distinguishable shapes and shows how much time remains.

After thought

Colorblind people are just a portion of minorities who have different kinds of difficulties. This means we have a lot to consider other than just traffic lights. For instance, we can create road lanes that are visible at night for people who have poor night vision. We must push ourselves and constantly develop our traffic system, until we design it to satisfy all the drivers including minorities and to help everyone to make a safe traffic system.
