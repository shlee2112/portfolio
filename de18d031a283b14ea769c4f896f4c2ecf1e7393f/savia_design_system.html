Savia Design System

A new design language for crafting clinically focused experiences

Overview

In the demanding hospital setting, clinicians frequently navigate vital patient and clinical data. However, our inflexible design assets failed to align with clinical protocols and lacked a clear visual hierarchy, impeding the user's ability to access critical information efficiently. As a result, I led the design team in developing a revolutionary design language tailored to empower clinicians, enabling seamless focus on crucial tasks and driving goal attainment. This involved defining foundational principles, establishing design tokens, and curating a library of reusable assets, fostering a unified and adaptable design approach.

Problems

Without a centralized collection of reusable components and guidelines, our products faced numerous issues. Therefore, I began shaping the future of our own design system to address the problems we identified.

question_answer

Inconsistent visual communication

The same or similar information was visually expressed differently in various applications due to the absence of standards. This inconsistency hindered a cohesive user experience across products.

ads_click

Unpredictable interactions and behaviors across all products

Because of inconsistent component behaviors, users often found it challenging to maintain focus and complete their tasks.

color_lens

Missing key visual elements that define brand identity

Despite having a suite of products, each product looked as if it were designed by a different company.

Design Approach & Strategy

To ensure interface elements are logically organized, directing users' attention to critical details first, I established four emphasis level rules

Through the strategic assignment of colors to each level, we achieved consistent and predictable interfaces.

arrow_downward

Foundations

Building upon the concept, I developed comprehensive guidelines for foundational elements including color, typography, spacing, motion, and more.

Design Tokens

I utilized foundational elements to establish design tokens, serving as the fundamental building blocks for all UI elements. Through the creation of global, semantic, and component tokens, I meticulously managed the consistency and scalability of the UI.

Components

Utilizing our unique design principles and foundational elements, I iteratively developed components designed to empower our users.

Development/Release Process

In order to establish a sustainable system, I proposed a development and release process collaboration between the development team and the design team. To ensure components are built according to the correct specifications and without any functional issues, we established separate environments for UI auditing and functional testing.

Documentation/Handoff Process

Prior to finalizing the asset handoff, I thoroughly document the full specifications of each component, including structure, styles, and animations, to better communicate with the dev team. Also, I maintain a detailed changelog to track the progression of changes over time.

UX Audits

Before releasing a new version, we conducted a thorough UX audit to ensure that all components were built correctly in a test environment.

Impact

After 8 releases of the design system, the most significant impact we achieved was the company's improved understanding of our design language. Also, through clear and intuitive visual communication and enhanced functionality, the design team was able to create cohesive and efficient experiences across all products.
