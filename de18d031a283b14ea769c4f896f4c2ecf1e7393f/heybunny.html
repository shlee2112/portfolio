Heybunny

Personalized newsletter reading app utilizing NLP capabilities

Overview

Starting as a team of four, we launched a new newsletter reading app called Heybunny, which utilizes NLP capabilities and helps users discover newsletters tailored to their personal preferences and read them without language barriers. In one year, it reached over 4K users and facilitated over 7K weekly reads, which was 20% more than our end of year goal.

As the lead designer, I spearheaded UX initiatives to expand the reach of the newsletter experience for Gen Z users, aiming to cultivate a habit of transforming everyday content into insights. Also, in my role as a data analyst, I analyzed user behavior patterns and identified distinct user groups using clustering algorithms. Furthermore, I developed a Heybunny user activity monitoring tool to track and monitor users' key activities, including account creation, reading, and subscribing.

Approach

Our team conducted research and analyzed data from the beta version to identify opportunity areas based on the given requirements.

newspaper

Newsletters

Based on our collected data, we found that foreign newsletters were the most translated content. Users rely on newsletters to stay informed and consume knowledge on a daily basis.

diversity_3

Millennials and Generation Z Users (MZ users)

According to our research and data analysis, our target demographic consists of individuals aged 25-34, who comprised 70% of our users.

subway

Reading During the Commute to Work

The peak time for reading newsletters was weekday mornings between 7-9 am, and it slowly decreased throughout the day. Based on this insight, we assumed our users read newsletters during their commute to work.

Strategy & Direction

Based on this strategy, we created a user journey map to gain a deeper understanding of our users.

After careful discussion and prioritization of the features and experiences listed in the user journey map, we developed MVPs.

local_library

Reading experience, rather than a translation service

Users want seamless reading experiences rather than newsletter translation services using our platform.

phone_iphone

Mobile-First Platform

One of the lock-in strategies we developed was implementing a recommendation system for our readers. Due to the absence of a centralized platform, users faced difficulty in finding newsletters of interest.

phone_iphone

Mobile-First Platform

Since we discovered that our users read during their commute to work, we realized that the new product must prioritize a mobile-first experience.
