Starting as a team of four, we launched a new newsletter reading app called Heybunny, which utilizes NLP capabilities and helps users discover newsletters tailored to their personal preferences and read them without language barriers. In 8 months, it reached over 4K users and facilitated over 7K weekly reads, which was 20% more than our end of year goal. And, by the end of 2022, it reached 20K+ users, and our users read 100K+ newsletters using our platform.

As the lead designer, I spearheaded UX initiatives to expand the reach of the newsletter experience for Gen Z users, aiming to cultivate a habit of transforming everyday content into insights. Also, in my role as a data analyst, I analyzed user behavior patterns and identified distinct user groups using clustering algorithms. Furthermore, I developed a Heybunny user activity monitoring tool to track and monitor users' key activities, including account creation, reading, and subscribing.

Approach

Twigfarm began as an NLP research firm and transitioned into creating digital products aimed at assisting people in daily communication.

Originally, Heybunny served as an email translation service. As they aimed to expand their original service and establish it as a profitable model, I joined the team to redefine the beta product.

Key Learnings

The peak time for reading newsletters was weekday mornings between 7-9 am, and it slowly decreased throughout the day. Based on this insight, we assumed our users read newsletters during their commute to work.

According to our research and data analysis, our target demographic consists of individuals aged 18-34, who comprised 70% of our users.

Create a solution to help MZ users read foreign newsletters and gather insights during their commute to work
