Spotlight

Designing for the management of patients with incidental liver lesions

Overview

Spotlight addressed workflow and follow-up challenges that the hospital faced when patients entered the healthcare system and an incidental lesion was spotted on their liver. Prior to this, these patients were overlooked, but with the new solution, they had a safety net to bring them back for further scans and follow-up appointments. A full-time employee utilized this tool, conducting numerous hands-on user experience interviews and implementing updates to the app through rapid releases.

It also served as a chance to establish and validate a new design language focused on empowering clinicians to efficiently prioritize critical actions.

Background

At Intermountain Health, clinicians use an evidence-based dashboard to oversee follow-up care for incidental liver lesions (ILLs) detected on CT scans. This system coordinates imaging, patient communication, and tracking with the aims of minimizing loss to follow-up, facilitating early detection, and showcasing value/return on investment (ROI).

Problem Scope

After conducting multiple rounds of interviews, gathering information, and reviewing user feedback, we gained a clear understanding of user needs and pain points. Collaborating with our product manager, we prioritized and narrowed down the issues to three that align with our business goals and are technically feasible:

quiz

Information Obscurity

The accumulation of new features had obscured essential information and actions, hindering user comprehension and efficiency. Also, the previous dashboard failed to effectively draw users' attention to critical information when needed.

ads_click

Interactive Element Ambiguity

The user interface lacked clear differentiation between static text and actionable components, leading to user confusion and the need for trial and error to discern interactive elements.

account_tree

Information Fragmentation & Complex Navigation

The insufficient organization and structure of information within the dashboard resulted in users navigating across various columns and undertaking multiple steps to input information, diminishing user efficiency.

Strategy & Design

At its core, we aim to create a new, consistent visual system that enables seamless focus on crucial tasks. Also, we focused on building efficient tracking and follow-up experiences to ensure users don't lose track of high-risk patients.

Our team underwent extensive iterations and validations in collaboration with the Intermountain ILL team.

We meticulously considered various scenarios to ensure seamless user adaptation and efficient utilization of the new designs.

The Solution

question_answer

Easier and faster communication experiences

A simple, customizable system helps users control and modify the status of each tracking category more easily and quickly. Additionally, the redesigned data entry experience streamlines the communication of the status of liver lesion patients among users.

ads_click

Stay focused with just one click

One simple click quickly enables users to narrow down a large set of tracking data into actionable items and stay focused on their goals. Users no longer need to scroll and click multiple times to find pending actions or discover pending tasks.

layers

A new visual system and hierarchy that feels familiar

The new interface elements are organized in a logical manner, directing users' attention to the most important details first. Also, by utilizing a color code similar to their EHR environment, users can notice critical and actionable information right away.

exposure_plus_1

The insights gained from this project significantly shaped the foundation of our new design language, fostering a cohesive visual identity across our organization. Additionally, it streamlined the development process for future apps by establishing a shared design language.
