Spotlight

Redesigning Incidental Liver Lesion (ILL) Dashboard

Overview

The redesign of Spotlight addressed numerous usability challenges stemming from the accumulation of new features. It also served as a pioneering application, establishing and validating a new design language focused on empowering clinicians to efficiently prioritize critical actions.

Role:

Lead Designer

Contribution:

Disclaimer: The patient details depicted in the designs are entirely fictitious and comply with all regulations regarding Protected Health Information (PHI).

Background

At Intermountain Health, clinicians use an evidence-based dashboard to oversee follow-up care for incidental liver lesions (ILLs) detected on CT scans. This system coordinates imaging, patient communication, and tracking with the aims of minimizing loss to follow-up, facilitating early detection, and showcasing value/return on investment (ROI).

Problem Scope

After conducting multiple rounds of interviews, our team identified 3 major problems:

quiz

Information Obscurity

The accumulation of new features had obscured essential information and actions, hindering user comprehension and efficiency. Also, the previous dashboard failed to effectively draw users' attention to critical information when needed.

ads_click

Interactive Element Ambiguity

The user interface lacked clear differentiation between static text and actionable components, leading to user confusion and the need for trial and error to discern interactive elements.

account_tree

Information Fragmentation & Complex Navigation

The insufficient organization and structure of information within the dashboard resulted in users navigating across various columns and undertaking multiple steps to input information, diminishing user efficiency.

Goals

We transformed our primary challenges into actionable opportunities to address during the redesign process

layers

Create visual hierarchy

Organize interface elements in a logical manner that directs users' attention to the most important details first.

ads_click

Empower user control

Create simple, customizable systems that users can control and modify.

ads_click

Create consistent UIs

The insights gained from this project significantly shaped the foundation of our new design language, fostering a cohesive visual identity across our organization. Additionally, it streamlined the development process for future apps by establishing a shared design language.
