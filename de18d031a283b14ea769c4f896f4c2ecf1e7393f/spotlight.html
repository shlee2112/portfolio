Spotlight tackles workflow and follow-up challenges that the hospital faced when patients entered the healthcare system and an incidental liver lesion was detected. Previously, these patients were often overlooked, but the new solution provided a safety net, ensuring they returned for additional scans and follow-up appointments. This solution has resulted in an annual ROI of $3 million and is saving countless lives every day.

As the lead product designer for this project, I led all design initiatives and conducted extensive user experience interviews. I also collaborated with a product manager, a designer, and developers to build and launch the AI-driven ILL patient dashboard.

Goals

At Intermountain Health, a group of clinicians launched a project to address gaps in clinical care and challenges in managing incidental liver lesions (ILLs). Approximately 68% of ILL patients didn't receive appropriate follow-ups within the first month due to over-diagnosis challenges faced by radiologists. To tackle this issue, they created an ILL patient management dashboard to oversee follow-up care for ILLs detected on CT scans.

After an internal pilot test confirmed its utility, the Intermountain ILL team asked Savia Health to develop a new dashboard with improved usability that better meets their goals.

Discover

To understand the Incidental Liver Lesion (ILL) workflow at Intermountain Health and identify usability challenges in their internal pilot test of the ILL patient dashboard, we conducted multiple rounds of virtual and in-person interviews.



Key Learnings

Follow-up Process and Workflow Insights

Our research provided insights into how incidental findings are managed upon detection in CT scans. We also identified key actions within the ILL Dashboard, tailored to specific follow-up recommendations and workflows.

Identified Usability Issues from Internal Pilot Test

To better understand the pilot test's outcomes, we observed clinicians using the dashboard and noted usability issues to assess user experience. We also facilitated focus group sessions, posing open-ended questions to discuss insights and challenges.

And, here are some key usability issues we identified:

Prioritization Challenges in Patient Follow-Up

The current design makes it difficult to prioritize patients' varying risk levels, complicating timely action. Also, managing patient follow-ups is hampered by the high volume of tasks and the absence of a clear visual aid or organized list.

"When I look at this, it feels overwhelming, and I don't know what I need to do."

Confusing Interfaces

Some actions or functions, like dropdowns or buttons, are not intuitive or visible, leading to missed features or added confusion

“How do you know which cell to click on?ˮ

Inadequate Communication Tracking

The system does not effectively track communication with patients or primary care physicians, making it difficult to follow up appropriately

“I put all my notes in this comment box… it's all manually typed."

Design Direction & Strategies

MVP & Prioritization

After careful discussion and prioritization of the features and experiences listed in the user journey map, we developed MVPs.

Design Iterations & Validations

At its core, we aim to create a new, consistent visual system that enables seamless focus on crucial tasks. Also, we focused on building efficient tracking and follow-up experiences to ensure users don't lose track of high-risk patients.

Our team underwent extensive iterations and validations in collaboration with the Intermountain ILL team.

We meticulously considered various scenarios to ensure seamless user adaptation and efficient utilization of the new designs.

Deliver

Communication Experiences

Easier and faster communication experiences

A simple, customizable system helps users control and modify the status of each tracking category more easily and quickly. Additionally, the redesigned data entry experience streamlines the communication of the status of liver lesion patients among users.

Most important detail and action first

A new visual system and hierarchy that feels familiar

The new interface elements are organized in a logical manner, directing users' attention to the most important details first. Also, by utilizing a color code similar to their EHR environment, users can notice critical and actionable information right away.

Stay focused with just one click

One simple click quickly enables users to narrow down a large set of tracking data into actionable items and stay focused on their goals. Users no longer need to scroll and click multiple times to find pending actions or discover pending tasks.

Consistent and predictable Interface

The insights gained from this project significantly shaped the foundation of our new design language, fostering a cohesive visual identity across our organization. Additionally, it streamlined the development process for future apps by establishing a shared design language.
