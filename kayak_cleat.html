Kayak Cleat


For Inexperienced Kayakers

Overview

Kayak Cleat changes actions and movement that inexperienced kayakers previously encountered, which cause danger and frustration. By serving as a handle and providing a hole that paddles can hook into, the kayak cleat contributes to a safer and larger accessible space for inexperienced kayakers.

Year: 2015

Duration: 4 months

Overall Process

Preliminary Research – Field Study – Opportunity – Approach – Ideation – Iteration - Deliverable

Preliminary Research

There are 215.8 million outdoors participants who come out for fishing, diving, wilderness exploration, touring, camping and recreational reasons and the number of water paddlers is increasing every year.

Problem Identification

Based on my research, I discovered that when individual inexperienced kayakers attempt to get out of the kayak and onto the dock, their instincts are to put their feet or upper body up first on the dock.

However, this is unsafe. It is a common mistake to fail to recognize that the kayak will move away from the dock. As they position their kayak along the dock, they instinctively feel it is stable. However, disembarking feet first can result in a serious injury.

Inexperienced kayakers get frustrated with something they don’t have control over. They make many attempts to place their kayak along the dock. They often bump into the dock or find themselves in an awkward place where it seems the dock is reachable with their hands.

Design Approach

To help kayakers safely disembark I have developed a cleat that will be placed on the dock. First, this can serve as a handle. Having something they can grab onto, we can change the steps or routine inexperienced kayakers use when disembarking. The first step would be to grab onto the cleat and pull themselves out.

I discovered that inexperienced kayakers would also try to reach over to the dock with their paddles, since the paddles are long and are the only controllable tool on their hands. Also, I found that people would only look for something that can hook their paddles onto to pull themselves toward the dock.

Therefore, I decided to create that can be compatible with paddles. Furthermore, where before the space along the dock was the only accessible area to dock, the product will help to create a larger accessible space. Therefore, it will lessen kayakers’ anxiety to have a perfectly timed turn or risk bumping into the dock.

Ideation

I made a hole for the kayak paddle that people can fit their paddles into. Moreover, I added inclined wings so that the paddles can slide into the hole. The form of the base came from the from of a kayak.

I placed indents on both sides, because the indent is not visible to the kayaker on water. Otherwise, they would not know there is an indent on the other side of the cleat.

Iterating & Refining

First Iteration

This first model has an indent, so once the kayak paddle fits into the cleat, the indent can hold the kayak. When a kayaker pulls his/her paddle toward him/her, it may be slip off from the cleat.

Second Iteration

First Iteration didn’t have a semiotic of form saying, “This is for a kayak paddle.” Therefore, I made a hole that people can understand that this hole can fit the paddle. Moreover, I made it thicker to make it look stronger and added inclined wings to help paddles to slide into the hole. Also, I tried to create the form of kayaks on the base of my iteration. I placed indents on both sides, because the indent was not visible to the kayaker on water.

Third Iteration

Based on my previous iteration, I have changed into an organic form to bring the form of water. Also, I paid more attention on the base to tell it is for kayakers. The hole got bigger for the paddle. Also, addressing the unexperienced paddlers who might have low accuracy on water, I made the wings got longer.

Fourth Iteration

I sharpened the curved edges to create the natural form of kayaks. Also, I raised the wings a bit for a better visual of hole and accuracy. This model is starting to tell it is for the kayakers. The hole of cleat is much closer to the ground in consideration of how low the kayakers are on the water. Also, I removed the base, which looked unstable.

Fifth Iteration

I made some big changes for this iteration. First, I lowered the wings, because it was dangerous for the people on dock. I try to create similar form I had previously. Also, I widened the hole and merged with the base so that there are continuous curves from the wings to the ground. This not only brings stability but also helps aluminum to flow into a cast. Whole form got much thicker, because it looked weak before.

Sixth Iteration

I soften the end of the wings and made it more organic so it looks less aggressive. The curves are much softer and changes less dramatically.

Final Deliverable

Kayak Cleat changes actions and movement that inexperienced kayakers previously encountered, which cause danger and frustration. By serving as a handle and providing a hole that paddles can hook into, the kayak cleat contributes to a safer and larger accessible space for inexperienced kayakers.
