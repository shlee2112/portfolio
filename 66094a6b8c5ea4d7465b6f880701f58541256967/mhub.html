MHub


2019 Google Design Exercise

Overview

At the University of Michigan, new students often struggle to understand the nature of the student organizations in a process of searching and browsing both online and offline. After conducting user interviews, as well as usability tests, I have identified three major problems in centralizing, discovering and understanding online information. With more than 1,400+ student organizations at Michigan, online information is outdated and scattered across many different platforms. Therefore, I designed a hub where new students can explore and discover their future student clubs, with aggregated images and posts from social media, and can pitch their new student group ideas.

Platform: Responsive Web Application

Year: 2019

Duration: 1 Week

Tools: Sketch, InVison, Illustrator

Overall Process

Problem Scope – User Interviews – Analysis – Brainstorming – Low-Fi Prototyping – Usability Tests – Refinement - Mid/High-Fi Prototyping - Deliverables

Design Brief

A new school year is approaching and the University of Michigan orientation team from the Office of New Student Programs is looking to you for some design expertise. Design an experience for new students to browse, search, and propose new student organizations.

Problem Scope

Identifying the possible users

Before taking any design process steps, I thought it was crucial to align myself with the context and the objective of the design exercise. First, I assumed I was designing for the Orientation Team from Office of New Student Programs, so I could use all accessible resources around me.

On the next step, I identified who I was designing for. Even though 70% of 9,500 total new students are undergraduates at Michigan, I didn’t limit myself to just designing for the new undergrads. Therefore, I decided to go deep into all incoming undergrad, master’s, and PhD students at the University of Michigan.

User Research

After identifying my users, I decided to conduct user interviews instead of surveys. Even though I knew I could gain a lot of data in a short time frame with surveys, I needed a deep understanding of users’ experiences in searching and proposing new student clubs. Therefore, I set up 6 user interviews: 2 from undergrad, master’s, and PhD programs.

During my interviews, I asked questions primarily focused on understanding a journey from being admitted to Michigan, the first day of orientation, to discovering and proposing new student organizations of their interests. I thought it was important to get a full scope of experiences behind searching for new student groups. Therefore, I decided to begin my questions from being admitted to Michigan. Also, I went deeper with any frustrations and challenges they have encountered during their journey with follow-up questions.

View User Research Questions

Analysis

After conducting five user interviews (one master’s interviewee dropped out due to illness), I took my time to listen to all of voice recordings of the interviews. Then, I began synthesizing and grouping overlapping findings and problems along the user journey. Every interviewee had a different but unique experience, but I was able to identify two overarching problems within a process of searching and proposing new student organizations.

  1. Scattered and outdated online Information

    “I never looked up MaizePages, because I knew there is no useful information I can find.”

    Information about UM student organizations is scattered all across platforms such as MaizePages*, Facebook pages, and their own websites. Therefore, new students had to hunt for information online.

  2. Difficult to find people with similar interests

    “Already had an interest and a purpose in climbing, so just had to find out about how to make it happen.”

    Even though there are more than 46,000 UM students walking around campus, students found it difficult to get more than 10 signatures to officially register a new student group.

View All Findings

Therefore, after aligning with users’ frustrations and challenges, I was able to focus on a clear problem statement to follow during my design process.

Problem Statement

At the University of Michigan, new students struggle to understand the nature of the student organizations in a process of searching and browsing both online and offline. Also, they often encounter challenges in finding students with similar interests.

Design Goal

Therefore, I decided to focus on designing a solution that helps

  • New students to search confidently
  • Student organizations to aggregate and manage information easily
  • Student leaders to make their student organizations successful and inviting for newcomers

Low Fidelity Prototyping

After brainstorming possible solutions, I started with sketches and began visualizing them using Sketch. The main purpose of low fidelity prototyping was to conduct usability tests and to validate my concept. Therefore, after finishing creating screens with Sketch, I used InVision to make them interactive for the usability tests.

  1. Home – Dashboard

    • On the Dashboard page, students can view their registered student organizations and their bookmarked student groups of their interests. Clicking the registered or saved student org directly leads to their student group page.
    • b. Students can see upcoming events of all student organizations to explore and discover unexpected student groups.

  1. Search Page

    • Most of the time, students already have an interest set in their mind before searching. Therefore, instead of showing all student organizations and filtering searches, the search page lets students filter first and view their interested student groups.
    • A unique hashtag aggregates all images and videos that students and student leaders post in their social network services such as Instagram, Facebook and YouTube.
    • Quick access features help students to see basic information before going to a student organization page.

  1. Student Organization Page

    • Bystander View – Students can take a glance at information, photos, and past activities of student organizations without signing up to those student organization.
    • Students can bookmark the student organizations of their interests into the Dashboard for coming back later.

  1. Register Page

    • Students can simply renew or register a new student organization to the University of Michigan by following easy steps.
    • New student organizations are assigned with a unique hashtag that allows to aggregate photos, videos, and posts from social media.

Usability Tests

Usability testing was the most critical and most challenging step during this exercise. After brainstorming, sketching, and creating low fidelity screens using Sketch, I needed to validate if I had correctly aligned with users’ needs. Therefore, I invited back 3 users from the previous interviews and conducted usability tests using InVision.

The usability tests primarily focused on validating the concept and the workflow of searching, browsing, and proposing organizations.

View Usability Test Questions

View InVision Prototype

After conducting 3 usability tests, I could find some positives and negatives.

Positives:

  • Interviewees appreciated how they could see upcoming events of all student organizations, since they sometimes make decisions by checking what activities the organizations are doing.
  • Interviewees saw a meaningful value with unique hashtags. When they had to hunt down photos or posts uploaded before, the hashtags allowed them to see all the activities and visual cues in a centralized place.

Negatives:

There were a lot of negatives about this prototype. However, among all the negative feedback, I want to focus on this quote from one of the usability tests:

“It is like Medium. Do you ever go to Medium to search for an article? You find it through Twitter, Facebook or something that someone has shared with you. It is same and secret to get attention from somewhere else.”

At first, I didn’t quite get what she was trying to say. However, I realized how my prototype has no reason or no strategy for the users to come back or get redirected to the web page. My biggest challenge was to get users to actively use this platform, so it becomes a living creature. However, I saw my prototype as just being another dead plant that could be outdated and could lack in information, like MaizePages. Therefore, when I faced this huge wall, I knew I had to step back and find missing pieces.

New Design Goal

After realizing the value of unique hashtags and the challenge in making my solution stay active, I stepped back and re-evaluated my interviews to find missing pieces. After listening to my recordings and synthesizing them again, I realized I was moving in the wrong direction. There was nothing wrong with my insights, overarching problems, and my problem statement. However, my design goals were too board and weren’t deeply touching the basis of user needs. Also, it was the moment I realized I tried to do everything fast in order to finish this exercise on time. Therefore, I took my time to rework on my design goals. And, finally to use a metaphor, I was driving a car with the right tires in the right direction.

A solution that helps

  • New students to search confidently
  • Student organizations to aggregate and manage information easily
  • Student leaders to make their student organizations successful and inviting for newcomers

A solution that

  1. Helps new UM students to discover and explore UM student organizations of their interests

    “Festifall happens only once, so everybody is going to be there. And, you know every option is there. You have a whole buffet.”

    Students appreciate offline events like Festifall, which gives an opportunity to explore and discover an unexpected nest of possibilities for new students

  2. Aggregates and manages all meaningful information about UM student groups to help students to understand their nature

    “I looked for information on the websites to prescreen the student organizations of my interest and went to Festifall to verify and build an interpersonal relationship.”

    Students need meaningful information online to help them understand and offline to build an interpersonal relationship. It is not about getting the necessary information to understand, but it is about being able to feel and imagine what it would be like to be in a particular student group.

  3. Becomes a centralized and living hub for all student organizations and students to share new student organization ideas and gather students with similar interests

    “We always have an area where that needs to be filled up by something that we don’t have. When we go into a new environment, we need a social place, like a student organization, that helps us to fill the gap we are missing. We either fill it up with similar interests we had before or replace it with something else.”

    The new students look for a place where they can feel a sense of intimacy. Also, most new students are anxious about adapting to a new environment and they need a safe space where they can share and talk with peers with similar interests.

Refinement

With my new design goal in my mind, I started from sketches to bring a new experience that fits users' needs:

Mid to High Fidelity Prototyping

Following my new design goal, I decided to design a responsive web application, which allows users to view it in different types of screens such as mobile and web. It gives a flexibility for the new students to not only search student organizations before coming to Ann Arbor but also actively look up while exploring Festifall.

Here are list features and functionalities that create an intended discovering and pitching experience for the new students and student groups.

  1. Login Page

    • UM Students login with their umich accounts to connect with UM peers and follow up with their student organizations ideas for the future. Also, UM Login provides two-factor login, which adds security.
    • b. Students can see upcoming events of all student organizations to explore and discover unexpected student groups.

  1. Home: Discover Page

    • All the highlights and recent social media posts of student organizations are shared with the new students. Therefore, UM Students can explore and discover unexpected student groups of their interest.
    • Students can view all the posts of a particular unique hashtag or particular student organization by simply clicking the hashtag and the name on the post. Also, basic information such as description, upcoming events, and contact information of the student organization is provided to the users.
    • Students can see all upcoming events happening on campus.
    • Students can check student organization idea proposals that have been pitched by UM students.

  1. Search Page

    • Students can search student groups of their interest.
    • All the categories are shown to the users to help understand the full scope of student organizations at the University of Michigan.
    • Students can filter their search by degree level to give a chance of finding peers with similar interests.
    • The most recent or highlighted posts by student organizations give a visual cue to the students to understand more intuitively about what the organizations do.

  1. Events Page

    • Students can see all the upcoming events with date, location, and affiliated student organizations.
    • Users can check past events to see if any interesting activities have happened.
    • Student can search or filter a particular event of their interest.

  1. Ideas Page

    • Students can propose their student organization ideas, as well as discuss and gather peers with similar interests.
    • Students can sign up to a particular idea of their interest and become part of any new student organization.

Accessible Design

I consider accessibility an important part of design. Especially, when I am leading 47 students to teach about web development and accessibility as a Graduate Student Instructor (TA), I have to make an input in designing for all users. Therefore, I make sure all the color contrast ratio is high enough to read. Also, I consider colorblind users to make sure all the buttons are readable without colors.


Lastly, in order to make my platform accessible to different types of screens such as mobile and desktop, I use a grid system to make my design responsive to the size of the screen.

Design System

One thing I learned over many years is the importance of making a consistent design that can embrace all different types of screens. Since I was designing for both mobile and web users, I had to make a small design system with foundation of typography, color, icons, and buttons sizes. I followed Perfect Fourth Scale (1.333) for the stability in type scale. I also tried to combine the identity of the University of Michigan and the identity of my application, which is a beehive. Then, I decided to use Icons from Material Design, which are already well established for all different platforms.

Final Deliverables

Mhub

MHub is a hub for new students who want to explore and discover their future student organizations. It is also a central place for incoming student groups where students share their student club ideas and meet peers with similar interests to launch a new student organization.

Reflections

A lot of rooms to improve, but…

One of the biggest challenges I encountered during this exercise was to stay focused on the mainstream of experience that users need but stay away from questions I could not answer. Even though it was only a week-long design exercise, I had a lot of questions raised in my mind. How can I handle the privacy issue? What is the better way to arrange SNS images and posts on the “Discover” page? If I were to actually develop this application, how I can promote it? Do I need a permission from the university? How should users interact with a mobile version? Even though I wanted to add more features on my application, I stayed on user insights and moved on according to my plan to finish on time.

Also, I wanted to understand how student organizations are managed. Therefore, I visited The Center for Campus Involvement who manages over 1,400 student organizations across the campus. However, I couldn’t get any opportunities to meet and chat with the staff members in a short time frame. Even though I was confident with the decisions I made throughout the process, it was tough to stay away from going further and diving deeper into my curiosity and questions I couldn’t answer.

Overall, this exercise was an opportunity to check how far and how deep I could go in a week. Also, I felt thankful to have this chance to be a part of 2019 Google Design Challenge.
